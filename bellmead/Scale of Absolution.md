A heavy brass balance scale mounted on a stone base, its surface worn smooth by centuries of ritual use. One pan is etched with prayers and invocations of mercy, the other scarred with tally marks, scratches, and old gouges where objects were placed and removed again and again. The arms of the scale tremble faintly, even when empty, as though remembering past weight.

No matter how carefully it is balanced, the scale never rests level for long.

_“If it does not tip, the burden has not been named.”_

#### Lore:
The Scale of Absolution was used by the Hollowed Saints to determine when a pilgrim’s grief could safely be released into the lake or passed onward to the cistern. In time, the Saints began using it on themselves.

The scale tipped more often.  
The entries in the Ledger grew longer.  
Nothing was ever light enough.

Some scratches on the brass are deeper than others, carved in moments of desperation when the scale refused to settle.

Father Lorian’s final note regarding the Scale is written in the margin of the Ledger:

> _“We mistook imbalance for sin.”_
