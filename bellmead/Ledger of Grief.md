A thick, iron-bound book swollen with age and moisture. Its pages are made of treated vellum, stiff and yellowed, each ruled with careful columns. Names fill every page. Some are written cleanly, others scratched out, overwritten, or circled again and again. Ink stains bleed through multiple sheets, as though certain griefs were too heavy to remain contained on a single page.

When opened, the air grows quieter. Candle flames nearby lean inward.

>_“What is written here was never meant to stay.”_
_ _"The ink may fade, but the sorrow remains."_

#### Lore
The Ledger of Grief was never intended as a permanent record. It was meant to be erased, emptied, and rewritten as sorrow passed through the Saints and was released. When the cycle broke, the Ledger filled faster than it could be cleared.  

Many entries end mid-sentence.  
Some names appear on every page.  
A few pages list no names at all, only weights.  

The final pages are blank, save for a single line written in Father Lorian’s hand:

> _“If we are still counting, we have failed.”_
