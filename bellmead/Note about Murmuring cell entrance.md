	


I have walked this corridor so many times that the saints no longer watch me.

At first I thought the likenesses were meant to accuse. Hands outstretched, faces hollowed, eyes cast downward in endless vigil. But the longer I remained, the more I noticed the fault in the pattern. One figure does not grieve as the others do.

Its stone is smoother. Less worn by fingers. The moss gathers there differently, as though it is disturbed and then allowed to grow again. When I knelt to pray, the drip of water behind it changed, just for a moment, as if the wall drew breath.

Father Lorian warned us not to linger. _“Meditation is not inspection,”_ he said. _“Confession should not seek answers.”_ Yet I have learned that stone remembers touch, and saints remember when they are handled with care rather than fear.

I think the passage was never meant to be hidden from the faithful. Only from those who rush past without listening.

Maybe it does not open to force. It yields to stillness.

It's a shame the well is sealed now... maybe whoever is down there now will discover the secret.
