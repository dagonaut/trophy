
Fashioned from rendered tallow, lake-wax, and Aelitha's relentless resolve, this candle burned through countless nights beneath the bell tower while she sought to cure the lake's sickness. Its wick is blackened and twisted, yet it never shortens, as if time refuses to claim it. It will only burn out when her task is complete.

"It burned as she sought the cure.
It burned as she was silenced.
It burned as she was banished.
It burned."

"They sealed her mouth, then they sealed her body, but the candle kept burning."

When lit, the flame burns steady and low, illuminating hidden markings, drowned truths, and echoes that cling to the world like scars. Even in silence, it hums faintly, as though listening.


Journal Entry:

I did not want to be there. They said they needed volunteers, so no one could claim ignorance. I said I was there just as a witness, I wouldn't lift a hand or a stone. 

She said nothing when we came for her. Just continued her work by her candlelight. Someone said it was a mercy to put her somewhere she could no longer frighten the lake. As we pulled her away the flame bent toward her like it wanted to followed. She looked back as if to apologize for leaving things unfinished. And when that iron seal was lowered, she looked at us... at me. Not with anger or fear, but resolve. She knew this would happen and what would happen next.

The Well started weeping that night and continues still. The others don't hear it, but I do. Try as they might, they couldn't silence her.

I don't know how long its been. When you can't sleep you can't count the days. I don't whose sobs come from the well, maybe they're mine. That ends tonight... and that damned candle still burns.

### **Journal Entry I — Aelitha’s Final Night**

_The candle will not go out._

I tried drowning it in wax tonight. I tried pinching the flame until my fingers blistered. It bends, but it does not die. Perhaps it understands what I cannot yet say aloud.

The echoes are clearer now. They gather near the bell’s shadow, like children afraid to speak first. The Lake is not hungry. It is clogged. Choked with what was given without consent.

If I am wrong, then tomorrow they will silence a fool.  
If I am right, then tomorrow they will silence the cure.

I have hidden the notes beneath the tower stones. The bell listens. The candle remembers.

---

### **Journal Entry II — Found Near the Bell Tower Stairs**

She did not scream when they told her.

That is the part I cannot forget.

The candle was still burning when they came for her, even as the rain soaked the stones. Someone said it was witchcraft. Someone else said it was mercy, to put her somewhere she could no longer frighten the Lake.

She looked at the tower once. Just once.  
As if to apologize for leaving it unfinished.

When they dragged her away, the flame bent toward her like it wanted to follow.

---

### **Journal Entry III — A Reluctant Hand**

_I did not want to be there._

They said every household must send someone, so no one could later claim ignorance. I told myself I was only a witness. That I did not lift a stone or speak a word.

But when the iron seal was lowered, she looked at us. At me.

Not with anger. Not with fear.

With relief.

As if this proved something she already knew.

The Well wept that night. I swear it did. And the candlelight from above did not fade until morning. They say she is still alive down there, still searching.

I hope she is wrong.  
Because if she is right, then we buried the only one who was listening.
