# 🜂 ECHO CLEANSING — GM QUICK REFERENCE

_Improvising Cleansing Moments in Jorando_

Use this sheet when the world feels heavy, unfinished, or wrong in a way that violence won’t solve.

You never announce a ritual.  
You describe a **pressure**, then watch what the players do.

---

## WHEN TO OFFER A CLEANSING MOMENT

- Players linger or hesitate
    
- A location feels wrong but not hostile
    
- An Echo reacts subtly
    
- A player speaks regret or doubt in character
    
- Silence settles at the table
    

If players rush or escalate to violence, **do not offer cleansing**.

---

## CORE QUESTION (Always Ask Yourself)

**What is unfinished here, and who is paying the price for it?**

---

## CLEANSING PROMPTS

### 1. THE PLACE THAT REMEMBERS INCORRECTLY

**Signs:**

- Misspelled names
    
- Incomplete murals
    
- Statues facing the wrong way
    

**Ask Yourself:**  
_What truth was simplified for comfort?_

**If an Echo Is Used:**

- The place corrects itself quietly
    
- A lie collapses into a smaller truth
    

**Cost:**  
Echo transforms into a mundane but heavy remnant

---

### 2. THE OBJECT THAT REFUSES USE

**Signs:**

- A door opens but won’t move
    
- A tool feels wrong in hand
    

**Ask Yourself:**  
_What grief blocks function, not access?_

**If an Echo Is Used:**

- The object works once, cleanly
    
- Then becomes inert or breaks
    

**Cost:**  
Echo loses relevance to similar situations

---

### 3. THE SPIRIT THAT IS NOT HOSTILE

**Signs:**

- A figure repeating a task endlessly
    
- A presence that watches but does not attack
    

**Ask Yourself:**  
_What does this spirit need permission to stop doing?_

**If an Echo Is Used:**

- The spirit dissolves peacefully
    
- Leaves behind silence, warmth, or dampness
    

**Cost:**  
Echo becomes less distinct, more generalized

---

### 4. THE ECHO THAT REACTS FIRST

**Signs:**

- Light flickers
    
- A distant sound answers
    
- A brief unrelated vision
    

**Ask Yourself:**  
_Why does this place recognize this Echo?_

**If an Echo Is Used:**

- Area changes permanently
    
- Future encounters here shift
    

**Cost:**  
Echo cannot be used the same way again

---

### 5. THE CHOICE BETWEEN REPAIR AND RETRIEVAL

**Signs:**

- Treasure inside a damaged structure
    
- Relic beneath corrupted water
    

**Ask Yourself:**  
_What breaks if they take this without mending what holds it?_

**If an Echo Is Used:**

- Retrieval is safe
    
- Space stabilizes or collapses harmlessly
    

**Cost:**  
Echo binds to the retrieved item

---

### 6. THE SILENCE THAT SHOULD BE SOUND

**Signs:**

- A bell that won’t ring
    
- A chapel without echo
    
- Mouths moving without sound
    

**Ask Yourself:**  
_What voice was removed rather than resolved?_

**If an Echo Is Used:**

- One sound occurs, once
    
- Silence becomes natural afterward
    

**Cost:**  
Echo loses communicative properties

---

### 7. THE MOMENT OF CONFESSION

**Signs:**

- A player admits regret or doubt in character
    

**Ask Yourself:**  
_What does the world do when truth is spoken freely?_

**If an Echo Is Used:**

- A burden lightens
    
- The environment responds subtly
    

**Cost:**  
Echo reflects the confession in some way

---

## WHEN **NOT** TO OFFER CLEANSING

- During fast-paced combat
    
- When danger must remain unresolved
    
- If it would trivialize a hard choice
    
- When players are disengaged
    

Cleansing thrives in **hesitation**, not urgency.

---

## FINAL GM PRINCIPLE

Echoes do not ask:  
**“Can you fix this?”**

They ask:  
**“Will you finish this?”**

Let the players decide where care is spent — and where it is withheld.
