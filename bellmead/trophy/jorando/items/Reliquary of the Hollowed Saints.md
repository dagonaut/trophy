_A heavy, sanctified vessel of darkened silver and stone, its surface etched with prayers worn smooth by centuries of touch. Cracks run through its casing like fault lines, and from within seeps a faint warmth, as if something inside is still breathing. Those who linger near it hear a low murmur: not a voice raised in sermon, but one caught forever mid-confession._

The Reliquary was never meant to be a prison. It was crafted to _hold_ grief briefly, to give sorrow shape so it could be named, weighed, and finally released. When Father Lorian realized the Hollowed Saints had misunderstood the rite, it was already too late. In an act of desperation, he bound his own Echo within the vessel, believing that by containing the error at its source he could halt the corruption spreading through the church and the cistern below.

Instead, his trapped Echo became an engine of imbalance. Lorian’s unending absolution, denied release, fed endlessly into the waters. The cistern did not cleanse sorrow anymore. It _circulated_ it.

The Reliquary grows heavier the closer it comes to water drawn from the Lake of Tears. It is said that if the Echo within is finally released where the waters first turned, the cycle may yet be broken.

> _“I did not take their grief to keep it. I took it so it could be let go. If you hear me still, then I have failed.”_
