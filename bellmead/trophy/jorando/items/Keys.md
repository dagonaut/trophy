
#### Inner Key
Key of Regret
Key of the Guilty

A long narrow brass key, slender and soft, its edges smoothed by years of worrying. It bears the impression of having once been set into something larger, the metal worn to a perfect fit. 

The metal seeks a partner.

When brought near iron, it grows warm.

**Use:**  
Fits precisely into the hollow of another key.

_Lore Quote:_

> “It was never meant to bear the weight alone.”
> "Its not the hiding place, but what is hidden within"
> "Regret does not travel, it settles."
> "A prison needs/seeks/desires a prisoner."
> "A prison is incomplete/nothing without a prisoner."
> "Not all prisons have walls."
> "Guilt and regret are prisons of the mind."

#### Outer Key
Key of Imprisonment
A thick iron key, heavy and unfinished, the size of a man's forearm. Its shaft is split lengthwise, the metal folded back on itself as if it were never meant to stand alone. When examined closely, the hollow along its spine is not damage, but design—precise and deliberate.

It is a tool, no more, no less. 

The key hums faintly when grief is near.

**Use:**  
Cannot be turned while incomplete.

_Lore Quote:_

> “It stayed where it was placed.”
> "A bell is nothing without a hammer (clapper)"
> “They did not destroy the lock. They hid the hand that could turn it.”
> "They chose not to destroy the lock, but rather hide the hand that could turn it."
> "A prison needs/seeks/desires a prisoner."
> "A prison is incomplete/nothing without a prisoner."


#### Combined Key
==Key of Forgetting==
Key of Unremberering
Key of the Oubliette

A brass key seated within iron, its purpose unmistakable. The soft metal disappears into the heavier frame, leaving no visible join. What was once worry-worn becomes hidden, absorbed into something meant to endure.

This key was not made to open doors, but to ensure they would never need to be opened again.

The Saints used it to seal places that frightened them, not by destruction, but by removal from memory. The chamber beneath the bell tower was one such place. So too was the woman who worked there. When the key turned, the passage did not collapse, nor did it scream. It simply ceased to matter.


**Use:**  
Opens the sealed door beneath the Weeping Well.

_Lore Quote:_

> “What waited was not strength, but completion.”
> =="An oubliette does not kill its prisoners. It waits for the world to forget them."==
> "Alitha was not silenced by chains or walls, but by the certainty that no one would come back for her.""
