## 🜂 The Rite of Reclamation (Stabilized Design)

### **1. Echoes as Transformative Relics**

This is a great call.

- An **Echo is used once for cleansing**
    
- When used, it **does not vanish**
    
- Instead, it becomes a _new thing_:
    
    - A quiet relic
        
    - A scar
        
    - A reminder
        
    - Or a distorted remnant
        

This means:

- Players are not “spending” story
    
- They are **changing** it
    
- The world remembers what they did
    

Mechanically, this gives you:

- One-use power with **permanent narrative residue**
    
- Justification for Echo-derived items, NPC reactions, or altered locations
    
- A way to show that healing always costs _definition_

### **2. Partial Completion and Moral Forks**

Letting the Rite be partially completed is absolutely the right move.

Key strengths here:

- The game does not demand “fix everything”

- A legitimate **heal vs profit** axis

- End states that feel earned, not optimized
    

You now have:

- A healed land with no saints
    
- A powerful reliquary in the wrong hands

All of those are valid endings.

## 🜂 What Makes a Harmony (Design Principle)

A Harmony is **not combination**.  
It is **mutual permission to conclude**.

To qualify:

1. The Echoes must share a thematic axis
    
2. Neither Echo dominates the other
    
3. The result must _reduce_ pressure, not increase it
    

Harmonies are **rare** and **optional**.
