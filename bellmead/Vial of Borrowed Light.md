A tear-shaped glass vial stoppered with wax and bound in a thin silver chain. Inside swirls a soft, warm glow, like candlelight seen through fog. The glass is warm to the touch, and when held close, breathing slows involuntarily. It smells faintly of incense and clean linen.

_“It is easier to carry what is already burning.”_

### **Effect**
When you unstopper the **Vial of Borrowed Light**, choose one:
- **Ease the Weight**  
    Remove 1 Ruin or ignore the effect of a burden for one scene.    
- **Steady the Faithful**  
    Gain advantage or reduced risk on a roll involving courage, ritual, or endurance.    

After use, the vial grows dim and cannot be refilled except by placing it back into the Nave tree or the Cistern waters.

### **Cost (Hidden Until Taken)**

The light inside the vial was never meant to leave the tree.

Once removed:
- The tree becomes aware of the loss.    
- The room subtly shifts toward hostility.    
- The party is now _noticed_ by the Nave.    

### **Lore**
The Hollowed Saints distilled moments of relief from those who could not yet release their grief. These small mercies were hung in the Nave as proof that the system worked.  

They were never meant to be kept.
